## Key Concepts

- **Tikanga Data** — Data ethics grounded in Te Ao Māori relational philosophy.
  Data is not a commodity to be extracted but a relationship to be honoured.
  Trust flows from ethical stewardship, not from compliance checkboxes.
- **Standards as Pit of Success** — Good standards make the right thing easy and
  the wrong thing hard. Standardise the 80% that is commodity; reserve flexibility
  for the 20% that differentiates.
- **Enterprise Anarchy** — Not chaos, but intentional architecture that distributes
  decision-making to domain teams within a framework of shared standards. The
  alternative to both central control and ungoverned autonomy.
- **Data Convergence** — The distinction between significant data (business-meaningful,
  governed, modelled) and non-significant data (operational noise, logs, metrics).
  Architecture effort should focus on significant data.
- **The Forgotten Question Mark** — "What does good look like?" — the question most
  organisations skip before solutioning. Agreeing on success criteria before building
  prevents expensive rework.
